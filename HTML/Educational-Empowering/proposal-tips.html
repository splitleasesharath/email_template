✓ Do's — What Works

1. Mention your schedule specifically
"I need a place Monday-Thursday for my consulting job in Manhattan"

2. Show you read their listing
"I noticed you prefer quiet professionals — I work in finance and keep regular hours"

3. Be ready to commit
"Looking for a 3-month initial arrangement with possibility to extend"

4. Ask one specific question
"Is the space available starting March 1st?"

✗ Don'ts — Common Mistakes

1. Generic copy-paste messages
"Hi, I'm interested in your place. Let me know."

2. Being vague about timing
"Maybe sometime next month or whenever"

3. Negotiating in first message
"Would you take 30% less?"

4. Writing too much
Keep it under 150 words

Example Winning Proposal

"Hi Sarah, I'm a marketing manager who commutes from Westchester to Midtown. Your Upper East Side studio would be perfect for my Monday-Thursday schedule. I noticed you mentioned preferring long-term guests — I'm looking for at least 6 months. I keep quiet hours and have references from my previous Airbnb stays. Would March 1st work as a start date? Thanks, Michael"

Result: Accepted within 2 hours
